Cloud




The first component of developing a cloud transition strategy includes identification of processes that ought to be transitioned over to the cloud operating model, versus those that should be maintained in their existing format. Identification of processes that are specific to a departmental level, versus those that touch an entire agency, is facilitated through SEMOSS by the ability to tag different sets of data and map through to their final touchpoints.

This process includes two metrics - integration of the process and maturity of the process. Processes that are more mature and more unified ought to be transitioned to SaaS (Software as a Service), while processes that are less mature and less unified are better candidates for IaaS (Infrastructure as a service). The Cloud consideration grid (Figure 1) applies standardized metrics to this analysis.

SEMOSS provides guidance in quantitatively evaluating a process's maturity and integration leveraging the corresponding systems that support those processes. By linking cloud strategy (the processes) through to the execution layer (systems) via the meta-model, and then loading the data, SEMOSS provides insights in quantitatively characterizing the processes that would be the best fit for a transition to cloud.
