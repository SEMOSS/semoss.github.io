SOA




Semoss provides guidance in both developing the business case for an enterprise to transition to Service Oriented Architecture (SOA), and ultimately provides guidance in developing a transition plan to eliminate point-to-point interfaces. The value of SOA from an enterprise perspective is clear - organizations that transition to SOA are able to save significant operating costs annually by removing potentially thousands of point-to-point interfaces, reduce data latency issues through multiple-hop data flows, and increase service level at minimal cost to the organization.

The case for transition to SOA is clearly demonstrated with the Semoss network graph showing how data in an enterprise flows from system to system. The first graph (Figure 1) shows the network of data within an enterprise. When converted to a balloon layout (Figure 2), it becomes apparent that for this particular piece of data, it is often required to pass through potentially four systems before it reaches the receiving system. Too, the interactivity of the nodes shows that often times they are data conflicts between various systems.

In deployment and in conjunction with thought leadership (the metamodel and base costing assumptions), Semoss is able to identify costs associate with the transition, sequence the transition from an investment prioritization perspective to yield the highest Return on Investment (ROI), and provide a timeline/schedule to suggest an optimum transition.
