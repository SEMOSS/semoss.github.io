    Determine health of applications

    Easily understand the health of your applications on our application healthgrid. It which measures and presents the business value, technical maturity, and financial coordinate of each of your applications to give you insight into which applications are healthy. Based on these measurements it gives leaders a clearer view of which applications to invest in or decommission.

    Hover over some of the bubbles to see which systems seem to be delivering most effectively. In this case, system 5 has very high sustainment costs but doesn’t seem to delivering business value equal to this amount of input. On the other side we see system 6 has even larger sustainment costs but one of the highest business values. In this particular case it might be a good idea to look into retiring system 5 unless changes can be made.

    Measure System Redundancy

    Redundancies exist at various levels in an enterprise. They increase the budget and reduce the ability of an enterprise to achieve the best ROI. Heat maps provide an easy way to see variation based on different factors. You can easily view which types of business processes are served by multiple applications or systems and where there could be room for improvement.

    Try filtering by different categories to understand where redundancies are located and what can be causing them.

    What are the changes in gene expression in the experiment?

    Let’s pretend we are a scientist who researches infectious diseases such as Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (also known as MERS). Through lab experiments, we took human immune system cells and exposed them to MERS. We then took two measurements – pre and post exposure – measuring the change in gene expression levels.

    Gene symbols are along the X axis and our two experiment measurements – pre and post exposure to MERS - along the Y axis. Each square contains a gene expression measurement.

    We want to filter our data to find genes of interest. Use the filters in the top corner to see which genes change the most and which have the strongest expression.

    What genes had the greatest change in expression?

    Parallel coordinates allow us to understand patterns in data and to focus on parts of a data set with a certain characteristics. In this case we are looking for the genes that have changed the most. Select the lines with the highest fold change to bring the genes that change the most into focus.

    What are the shared pathways?

    We now have a network graph with the genes with the most change in expression. By double clicking on a gene or the top left you can explore to see what else they might share in common.
