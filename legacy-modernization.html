Legacy Modernization




Organizations use Semoss to better understand the technical makeup of each of their systems and to provide guidance in identifying systems for removal by demonstrating potential ripple effects throughout the enterprise. Deployed with thought leadership to develop the meta-model, the platform optimizes enterprises by linking strategy to execution, thus providing recommendations on how to modernize technology to better align with business objectives.

By connecting capabilities to systems, Semoss can first characterize the enterprise (e.g. identify the systems that support given business processes), and then provide investment guidance in targeting systems that are candidates for elimination, consolidation or replacement based on technical and programmatic requirements. Quantitative analytics, such as a system's business value, can deployed through Semoss to objectively evaluate systems from a comparative perspective.

Consider, for example, the application health grid, which quantitatively evaluates a system's business value and technical maturity. System's with the highest business value (most business processes supported) are considered more valuable to the enterprise and should be considered for adoption, while systems with low business value and low technical maturity are candidates for consolidation because they provide little value to the enterprise.
